Challenges

Public Version to solicit comments till Jan 15 at Google Doc.

Track #1: Driving with Language

Incorporating linguistic information, DriveLM connects Large Language Models (LLM) and autonomous driving systems, and eventually introduces the reasoning ability of LLM to make decisions and ensure explainable planning. Given multi-view images as inputs, models are required to answer questions covering various aspects of driving.

Track #2: CARLA Autonomous Driving Challenge

To verify the effectiveness of AD systems, we need an ultimate planning framework with a closed-loop setting. The CARLA AD Leaderboard challenges agents to drive through a set of predefined routes. For each route, agents will be initialized at a starting point and directed to drive to a destination point, provided with a description of the route through GPS style coordinates, map coordinates or route instructions. Routes are defined in a variety of situations, including freeways, urban areas, residential districts and rural settings. The Leaderboard evaluates AD agents in a variety of weather conditions, including daylight scenes, sunset, rain, fog, and night, among others.

Track #3: End-to-End Driving at Scale

Compared to the nuPlan Challenge 2023, this track prohibits direct access to map information. The newly designed PDM simulation and scoring go beyond open-loop evaluation for end-to-end autonomous driving frameworks.

Track #4: Predictive World Model

Serving as an abstract spatio-temporal representation of reality, the world model can predict future states based on the current state. The learning process of world models has the potential to elevate a pre-trained foundation model to the next level. Given vision-only inputs, the neural network outputs point clouds in the future to testify its predictive capability of the world.

Track #5: Occupancy and Flow

The representation of 3D bounding boxes is not enough to describe general objects (obstacles). Instead, inspired by the concept in Robotics, we deem general object detection as an occupancy representation to cover more irregularly shaped objects (e.g., protruding). The goal of this task is to predict the 3D occupancy of the complete scene and the flow of the foreground objects given the input image from six cameras.

Track #6: Mapless Driving

AD without HD maps demands a higher level of active scene understanding. This challenge aims to explore the boundaries of scene reasoning capabilities. Neural networks take multi-view images and Standard-definition (SD) Map as input, then provide not only perception results of lanes and traffic elements but also topology relationships among lanes and between lanes and traffic elements simultaneously.

About

Welcome to the CVPR 2023 Workshop on End-to-End Autonomous Driving!

This workshop aims to bring together leading researchers and practitioners to discuss upcoming paradigms for autonomous vehicles. Central to the program is a series of invited talks and three new challenges in the self-driving domain. Each challenge combines aspects of multiple subcomponents of traditional driving stacks, e.g. joint perception and prediction or joint prediction and planning. Winners of the challenges will present their results and insights as part of the workshop. We invite researchers around the world to build new algorithms to tackle these challenging, real-world autonomous driving tasks!

    OpenLane Topology Challenge

    The challenge of this dataset includes not only detecting l ane centerlines and traffic elements but also recognizing the attribute of traffic elements and topology relationships on detected objects.

    Open Time: 1 Apr 2023
    Close Time: 30 May 2023




    End-to-end Autonomous Driving Challenge

    nuPlan is the world's first large-scale planning benchmark for autonomous driving. We provide a large-scale dataset with 1300h of human driving data from 4 cities across the US and Asia with widely varying traffic patterns (Boston, Pittsburgh, Las Vegas and Singapore).

    Open Time: 1 Apr 2023
    Close Time: 30 May 2023




    Occupancy Prediction Challenge

    The goal of this task is to predict the 3D occupancy of the complete scene and the flow of the foreground objects given the input image from six cameras. The complexity of this task lies in the dense prediction of 3D space given the input of image, and the flow state of foreground objects also needs to be inferred.

    Open Time: 1 Apr 2023
    Close Time: 30 May 2023

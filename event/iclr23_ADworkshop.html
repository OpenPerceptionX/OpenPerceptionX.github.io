Scene Representation for Autonomous Driving

Workshop Series on End-to-end Autonomous Driving
In conjunction with ICLR 2023, May, Kigali City, Rwanda, Africa

The 1st International Workshop on Scene Representations for Autonomous Driving

Autonomous Driving (AD) is a complex research area. Through the aid of Machine Learning (ML) techniques, AD systems have rapidly developed, yet there are several challenges involved in deploying these ML algorithms to systems in production. Solving these challenges is beyond the capability of any single company or institution, necessitating large-scale communication and collaboration. Our goal is to provide a platform for this at ICLR, and promote the real-world impact of ML research toward self-driving technology. To this end, we propose a workshop titled “Scene Representations for Autonomous Driving” (SR4AD). We welcome contributions to this workshop in the form of oral presentations, posters and blogs. Suggested topics include, but are not limited to:

  • - Fresh perspectives on perception with corresponding tasks and algorithms, such as bird`s-eye-view representations, 3D road topology prediction, and occupancy networks.
  • - The role of large-scale data in determining the choice of algorithms and intermediate representations, with a particular interest in data-driven planning for AD.
  • - What is the desirable paradigm for AD systems? Is it a modular design with explicit intermediate representations or a unified perception and decision-making framework?
  • - Would large pretrained models be beneficial for applications in AD? Is there an intermediate representation that would enable a GATO/CLIP/etc. for this domain?
  • - What are the biggest challenges for deployable L2/L4 systems in the industry? Are they solvable from the academic side?
